Two delivery types — already built

🏫 Classroom Equivalent

Forced chapter progression. Quiz per chapter (70% to pass). Reading screens are timed. No final exam.

✓ Covers classroom minimum required by most states.

3 of our 5 courses use this format.

📖 Self-Study

Self-paced. Single final exam (min 10 questions per credit hour, 70% pass). No proctoring required in most states.

✓ Fills the remaining required hours.

2 of our 5 courses use this format.

The split is already designed in. No decisions needed — it works out of the box.

Question requirements — verify before writing

⚠️ Question bank size

Many states require a randomized bank 2–3× larger than the exam shown (e.g., 162 questions written to show 54). Not yet confirmed for TX.

Verify with TDI before writing — could multiply workload significantly.

⚠️ 11 states require proctored exams

CT, MA, DC, WV, GA, AL, MS, AR, IA, NV, HI require self-study finals monitored by a disinterested third party.

Platform issue, not content — must be resolved before launching self-study in these states.

Iowa: closed-book only

Every other state allows open-book exams. Iowa requires closed-book. IA questions must be knowledge-based, not look-up style.

Write IA exam questions separately when that state is built.

Classroom quiz minimums

TDI and most states don't specify a minimum per checkpoint quiz. Our 10 questions/chapter is a design choice, not a regulatory floor.

Could reduce to 5/chapter if LX capacity is a concern.

What "flexible delivery" means

Split-mandate states (TX, NJ, VT, NC, UT)

Regulations require at least 12 hrs classroom equivalent, max 12 hrs self-study. No flexibility — set by law.

Tier 2 — No split mandate (28 states)

No ratio required. All 24 hours could be self-study, all classroom, or any mix.

We use the TX split as default — already built, satisfies every requirement. Adjustable per market if needed.

Why this approach works

Build once

Source courses are never modified. Every state is a configuration — a manifest saying which chapters to include. No duplicate content, no diverging versions.

Scale fast

39 states share the same build steps as TX. Once TX is validated, the playbook repeats with minimal additional LX effort.

Future-proof

13.5 hrs of content held in reserve. When a state needs more hours, we activate chapters — we don't start over.

Low content risk

The vast majority of states need only generic content we already have. State-specific investment is targeted and high-ROI.

The bottom line

39 states ready with existing content
0 new courses needed for Phase 1 & 2
1 build pattern, replicated everywhere

Texas is the proof of concept. Once approved, we execute the same playbook across the country.
