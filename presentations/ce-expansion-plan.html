Two delivery types — already built

🏫 Classroom Equivalent

Forced chapter progression. Quiz per chapter (70% to pass). Reading screens are timed. No final exam.

✓ Covers classroom minimum required by most states.

3 of our 5 courses use this format.

📖 Self-Study

Self-paced. Single final exam (min 10 questions per credit hour, 70% pass). No proctoring required in most states.

✓ Fills the remaining required hours.

2 of our 5 courses use this format.

The split is already designed in. No decisions needed — it works out of the box.

Question requirements — verify before writing

⚠️ Question bank size

Many states require a randomized bank 2–3× larger than the actual exam shown (e.g., 162 questions written to deliver a 54-question exam). Not yet confirmed for TX.

Verify with TDI before writing questions — could multiply the workload significantly.

⚠️ 11 states require proctored exams

CT, MA, DC, WV, GA, AL, MS, AR, IA, NV, HI require self-study final exams to be monitored by a disinterested third party.

Platform/delivery issue, not a content issue — but must be resolved before launching self-study in these states.

Iowa: closed-book only

Every other state allows open-book self-study exams. Iowa requires closed-book.

Questions for IA must be knowledge-based — no "look it up" style questions. Write IA exam questions separately.

Classroom quiz minimums

TDI (and most states) don't specify a minimum question count per chapter quiz. Our standard of 10 questions per checkpoint is a design choice, not a regulatory floor.

Could be reduced to 5 per checkpoint if LX capacity is a concern.

What "flexible delivery" means

Split-mandate states (TX, NJ, VT, NC, UT)

Regulations require: at least 12 hrs classroom equivalent, max 12 hrs self-study.

No flexibility — the ratio is set by law.

Tier 2 — No split mandate (28 states)

No ratio required. All 24 hours could be self-study, all classroom, or any mix.

We use the TX split as default — already built, satisfies every requirement. We can adjust per market if needed.

Why this approach works

Build once

Source courses are never modified. Every state is a configuration — a manifest that says which chapters to include. No duplicate content, no diverging versions.

Scale fast

39 states share the same build steps as TX. Once TX is validated, the playbook repeats with minimal additional LX effort.

Future-proof

13.5 hrs of content held in reserve. When a state needs more hours, we activate chapters — we don't start over.

Low content risk

The vast majority of states need only generic content we already have. State-specific investment is targeted and high-ROI.
