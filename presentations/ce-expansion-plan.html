Two delivery types — already defined

🏫 Classroom Equivalent

Forced chapter progression. Embedded quiz at the end of each chapter (70% to pass). Reading screens are timed. No final exam.

Counts toward classroom minimum most states require.

📖 Self-Study

Self-paced. Single final exam at the end (min 10 questions per credit hour, 70% pass). No proctoring required.

Counts toward self-study allowance (typically up to 12 hrs).

For most states, our 3 classroom equivalent courses cover the minimum requirement. Our 2 self-study courses fill the remainder. The split is already built.

What "flexible delivery" means for Tier 2

Some states (TX, NJ, VT, NC, UT) mandate a specific split — e.g., at least 12 hours must be classroom equivalent.

Tier 2 states have no such mandate. A licensed producer could take all 24 hours as self-study, or all as classroom equivalent, or any mix.

What this means for us:

We use the TX split (13 hrs classroom / 11 hrs self-study) as our default for all Tier 2 states — it's already built and it satisfies every requirement. But if a state's market demands a different approach, we have room to adjust without rebuilding from scratch.

Why this approach works

Build once

Source courses are never modified. Every state build is a configuration — a manifest that says which chapters to include. No duplicate content.

Scale fast

39 states with identical build steps. Once TX is validated, the playbook is repeatable with minimal additional LX effort.

Future-proof

~13.5 hrs of content held in reserve. When a state needs more hours, we add chapters — we don't start over.

Low content risk

The vast majority of states need only generic content we already have. State-specific investments are targeted and high-ROI.
