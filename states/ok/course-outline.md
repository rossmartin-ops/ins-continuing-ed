## Chapter 1: Producer Conduct — New Standards and Enforcement (~5,000 words)

This chapter covers the biggest behavioral changes for producers: the annuity best interest standard, anti-rebating enforcement, and carrier compensation practices.

### 1.1 Annuity Best Interest Standard (OAC 365:25-17, effective Sept 1, 2023)
- What changed: Oklahoma adopted NAIC Model #275 — became the 40th state
- The four producer obligations: Care, Disclosure, Conflict of Interest, Documentation
- What "best interest" means in practice vs. prior suitability standard
- Training requirements: who must complete the 4-hr course and by when
- Documentation obligation: what records producers must keep and why
- Commissioner Mulready's statement on protecting retirees
- Practical scenarios: when care and disclosure obligations are triggered

### 1.2 Anti-Rebating — OID Enforcement (Bulletin 2025-01)
- What anti-rebating laws prohibit under 36 O.S. § 1204(8)(a)
- What OID found: commission return schemes being used to secure/retain business
- Why anti-rebating protects consumers (uniform pricing principle)
- Common prohibited arrangements producers must avoid
- Statutory exceptions under 36 O.S. § 1204(8)(b)

### 1.3 Carrier Commission Practices — Unfair Trade Practices (Bulletin 2025-12)
- What OID identified: carriers eliminating commissions mid-year, restricting enrollment
- Legal basis: 36 O.S. § 1209(A) unfair methods of competition
- What carriers cannot do without advance notice
- Producer rights and reporting options when carriers act improperly

### 1.4 Artificial Intelligence in Insurance (Bulletin 2024-11)
- OID's Written AI Systems Program requirement for carriers
- What producers need to know: carrier responsibility for AI-driven decisions
- Unfair Trade Practices and Unfair Claims Settlement compliance obligations apply to AI
- Producer role: identifying and reporting AI-driven discriminatory outcomes

## Chapter 2: Data Security — New Compliance Obligations (~4,500 words)

The Insurance Data Security Act is the most significant new compliance requirement for non-exempt licensees.

### 2.1 Insurance Data Security Act — Overview (SB 543, effective July 1, 2024)
- What it is: Oklahoma's adoption of NAIC Model Law #668
- Who is covered: all Title 36 licensees including producers
- Compliance timeline: July 1, 2025 (core program); July 1, 2026 (advanced requirements)

### 2.2 Exemptions — Does This Apply to You?
- Exemption for licensees with <$5 million gross annual revenue
- Exemption for employees covered by employer's program
- Other exemptions (HIPAA, GLBA-compliant programs)
- What happens if you lose exempt status (180-day window)

### 2.3 Core Obligations for Non-Exempt Producers
- Written Information Security Program: what it must cover
- Written Incident Response Plan requirement
- Third-party vendor oversight: due diligence and contract requirements
- Record retention: minimum 5 years

### 2.4 Cybersecurity Event Notification
- When to notify the Commissioner: 3 business days, when 250+ OK consumers affected
- Required notification content
- Consumer notification obligations (Oklahoma Security Breach Notification Act)
- Producers of record: carriers must notify you when your clients are affected

### 2.5 Annual Requirements
- Annual Board Report (for entities with boards of directors)
- Annual Data Security Attestation to OID
- Confidentiality of submitted records

## Chapter 3: Property and Casualty Updates (~3,500 words)

### 3.1 Assignment of Benefits Prohibition (HB 1084, effective Nov 1, 2025)
- What the law prohibits: soliciting or accepting AOB on property claims
- Covered policy types: auto collision/comp, residential property, commercial property
- Why this matters: addresses contractor and body shop fraud schemes
- Consequences: agreement is null and void
- Permitted exceptions (mortgagees, financial institutions, subsequent purchasers)
- Producer obligations: cannot facilitate AOB arrangements

### 3.2 Strengthen Oklahoma Homes Act (HB 3089, effective Nov 1, 2024)
- What the program is: grants for home retrofits to resist weather damage
- Eligibility and process: 3 competitive bids from certified contractors
- How to inform clients: potential premium reduction opportunities

### 3.3 Vehicle Storage Rate Caps (SB 641, effective Nov 1, 2025)
- New maximum storage rates for total loss vehicles
- Days 1–10: $39/day standard; $125/day lithium-ion battery
- Day 11+: $75/day standard; $200/day lithium-ion battery
- Administrative charge caps
- Producer impact on auto claims handling guidance

### 3.4 Workers' Compensation — First Responder Mental Injury (effective Jan 1, 2025)
- Full-time firefighters, paramedics, and police officers may file WC claims for mental injury
- Does NOT require a physical injury to trigger coverage
- New exposure category for municipalities and public safety employers
- Impact on commercial lines producers writing WC for government entities

## Chapter 4: Life, Health, and Medicare Updates (~4,000 words)

### 4.1 Medicare Supplement — Birthday Rule (OAC 365:10-5-129(f), effective Sept 1, 2023)
- What the birthday rule is: annual 60-day guaranteed-issue window
- Who it applies to and what plans qualify
- Medical underwriting waived when switching to comparable or lesser benefits
- Disability enrollee notification: 60–90 days before 65th birthday
- Selling opportunity vs. anti-twisting obligation: replacement rules still apply

### 4.2 2025 Health Insurance Consumer Protection Laws
- **SB 1050:** Claims refund windows shortened (claimants: 12→6 months; providers: 18→12 months)
- **SB 1019:** No time limits on covered anesthesia services
- **HB 1389:** Contrast-enhanced mammograms and supplemental screenings mandated
- **SB 109:** Genetic testing and cancer imaging coverage required
- **HB 1516:** Minimum contracting age for insurance raised from 15 to 16
- **SB 176:** 6-month birth control supply from single pharmacy pickup
- Producer impact: explaining these expanded coverages to health insurance clients

### 4.3 Prior Authorization Transparency (HB 1808, effective Nov 1, 2025)
- PA requirements must be published in plain language on plan website
- 60-day advance notice required for PA changes to chronic condition patients
- Decision timelines: 24 hours (urgent), 4 business days (non-urgent)
- Chronic condition PA approvals valid 3 years unless criteria change
- Penalty: up to $5,000 per violation

### 4.4 Mental Health Parity — Claim Review Standards (SB 557, effective Nov 1, 2023)
- MH/SUD claim reviewers must have appropriate, specialized credentials
- Generalist reviewers no longer sufficient for mental health claim denials
- Producer role in helping clients navigate MH/SUD claim disputes

### 4.5 Health Care Price Negotiation (SB 515, effective Nov 1, 2025)
- Enrollees may negotiate directly with providers for lower prices
- Full out-of-pocket cost counts toward deductibles and OOP maximums
- Providers accepting direct payment cannot balance bill
- How to explain this option to health insurance clients

### 4.6 Oklahoma State-Based Exchange Transition
- Target May 1, 2026: OID assumes program administration from CMS
- Full SBE operation target: November 1, 2027 (for 2028 Open Enrollment)
- What changes for producers: increasing OID interaction vs. CMS for Marketplace issues
- Navigator and assister programs will be Oklahoma-run

## Chapter 5: Licensing, CE, and Administrative Updates (~500 words)

### 5.1 Earthquake CE Requirement Eliminated (Bulletin 2023-05, effective Sept 1, 2023)
- Previously required earthquake CE course has been removed from the catalog
- No action required — this is a reduction in requirements

### 5.2 Short-Term Limited Duration Insurance — Extended Duration Permitted
- Federal non-enforcement of 2024 STLDI restrictions
- Oklahoma STLDI: up to 36 months total duration per 36 O.S. § 4419(A)
- Carrier must still obtain OID approval through SERFF before selling
- Producer obligation: verify carrier compliance before selling

### 5.3 Surplus Lines — SLIP Platform (effective January 1, 2024)
- All surplus lines policies effective on or after Jan 1, 2024: use SLIP (not OPTins)
- Premium taxes: 6% surplus lines tax + 0.175% SLAS transaction fee
- Electronic filing required; invoices quarterly via SLIP inbox

### 5.4 Licensing Updates
- Exam vendor changed to PSI (February 15, 2023)
- Exam fee reduced to $38 (August 1, 2024)
- Spanish-language exams now available (January 1, 2025)
