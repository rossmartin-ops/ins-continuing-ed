## SECTION 1: OKLAHOMA INSURANCE STATUTES — RECENT CHANGES TO TITLE 36

### 1.1 ANNUITY SUITABILITY AND BEST INTEREST STANDARD (2023)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2023-06; OAC 365:25-17 (amended); OID Press Release, August 24, 2023
**Effective:** September 1, 2023
**Oklahoma became the 40th state** to adopt the NAIC's revised Suitability in Annuity Transactions Model Regulation (Model #275).

**Core Standard:** Producers must "act in the best interest of consumers under the circumstances known at the time the recommendation is made." Four obligations:
- **Care** — Act in the consumer's best interest, without placing producer's financial interest ahead of consumer's
- **Disclosure** — Prior to recommendation, disclose producer/insurer relationships, compensation types, and material conflicts
- **Conflict of Interest** — Identify, disclose, and manage conflicts so they do not influence recommendations
- **Documentation** — Maintain records sufficient to show compliance

**Producer Training Mandates (OAC 365:25-17-7.1):**
- New sellers (no prior annuity history before Sept 1, 2023): 4-hour approved annuity training within 6 months of Sept 1, 2023
- New life license holders (on or after Sept 1, 2023): cannot sell annuities until 4-hour course completed
- Existing sellers who completed prior OID-approved training before Nov 1, 2023: must complete new 4-hr course OR 1-hr update course by April 30, 2024

### 1.2 INSURANCE DATA SECURITY ACT — SB 543 (2024)
**Source:** 36 O.S. §§ 670–679; OID Bulletin No. 2024-10 (Revised June 27, 2025)
**Effective:** July 1, 2024 (compliance staggered through July 1, 2026)

Oklahoma adopted NAIC Model Law #668 (Insurance Data Security Model Law). Establishes exclusive state data security standards for all Oklahoma insurance licensees.

**Who is covered:** All individuals and entities licensed under Title 36, including producers.

**Exemptions:** Licensees with <$5M gross annual revenue; entities subject to HIPAA with compliant programs; employees covered by employer's program.

**Core obligations for non-exempt licensees:**
- Written Information Security Program based on risk assessment
- Written Incident Response Plan
- Annual Board Report (first due July 1, 2025)
- Annual Data Security Attestation to OID (first due July 1, 2025; then annually by April 15)
- Third-Party Vendor Oversight with audit rights in contracts
- Cybersecurity Event Notification to Commissioner **within 3 business days** when 250+ OK consumers affected
- Record retention: minimum **5 years**

**Compliance deadlines:** Full compliance July 1, 2025; advanced requirements July 1, 2026.

### 1.3 MENTAL HEALTH PARITY — SB 557 (2023)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2023-11; 36 O.S. § 1250.5(10)
**Effective:** November 1, 2023

When any MH/SUD claim is under review, the reviewing provider must have appropriate, qualified, and specialized credentials relevant to those services. Generalist reviewers no longer sufficient for mental health claim denials.

---

### 1.4 ELECTRONIC PROVIDER DIRECTORIES — SB 442 (2023)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2023-13; 36 O.S. § 6971
**Effective:** November 1, 2023

Health benefit plan insurers must publish and maintain electronic provider directories updated every **60 days**.

---

### 1.5 STEP THERAPY AND BIOMARKER TESTING (2023–2024)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2023-04; 36 O.S.
- **HB 1736 (Nov 1, 2023):** Exception process required for step therapy in COPD/mechanical ventilation
- **HB 2748 (Nov 1, 2023):** No step therapy for prescription drugs treating advanced metastatic cancer
- **SB 513 (Jan 1, 2024):** All health benefit plans must cover biomarker testing when it provides clinical utility

---

### 1.6 2024 OMNIBUS INSURANCE BILL — HB 3091 (2024)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2024-08; 36 O.S. multiple sections
**Effective:** November 1, 2024

- Independent hearing examiners may now impose penalties (in addition to Commissioner)
- Minimum penalty requirement removed — penalties fully discretionary
- Surplus lines electronic filings required
- Surplus lines due diligence not required for exempt commercial purchasers (with written disclosure)

---

### 1.7 ASSIGNMENT OF BENEFITS PROHIBITION — HB 1084 (2025)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2025-07; 36 O.S. § 1230(B)
**Effective:** November 1, 2025

Prohibits soliciting or accepting assignments of post-loss insurance benefits for property damage under:
- Auto collision and comprehensive policies
- Residential property policies
- Commercial property policies

Violations: agreement is null and void. Addresses contractor/body shop fraud schemes.

**Exceptions permitted:** Assignments to federally insured financial institutions, mortgagees, subsequent property purchasers, and liability coverage remain valid.

---

### 1.8 2025 HEALTH INSURANCE CONSUMER PROTECTION BILLS
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2025-05
**Effective:** November 1, 2025

- **HB 1389:** Mandates coverage for contrast-enhanced mammograms and supplemental breast cancer screenings for high-risk individuals
- **HB 1516:** Minimum age to contract for insurance raised from 15 to **16 years** (with parental consent)
- **SB 109:** Health plans must cover clinical genetic testing for inherited cancer gene mutations without cost-sharing
- **SB 176:** Patients may obtain a **6-month supply** of birth control in a single pharmacy pickup
- **SB 1019:** Prohibits time limits on covered anesthesia services during medical/surgical procedures
- **SB 1050:** Reduces claims refund window — claimants from 12 to **6 months**; providers from 18 to **12 months**

---

### 1.9 PRIOR AUTHORIZATION TRANSPARENCY — HB 1808 (2025)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2025-04; 36 O.S. §§ 6570.50–6570.59
**Effective:** November 1, 2025

- PA requirements must be published in plain language on plan website
- PA changes: **60 days' notice** to providers and chronic condition patients
- Decision timelines: **24 hours** (urgent); **4 business days** (non-urgent)
- Chronic condition PA authorizations valid for **3 years** unless criteria change
- Penalty: up to **$5,000 per violation**

---

### 1.10 HEALTH CARE PRICE NEGOTIATION — SB 515 (2025)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2025-10; 36 O.S. (new)
**Effective:** November 1, 2025

Allows enrollees to negotiate directly with providers for lower prices than their health plan's average allowed amount. Full out-of-pocket amount counts toward deductibles and annual out-of-pocket maximums.

---

### 1.11 AMBULANCE SERVICE REIMBURSEMENT — SB 1067 (2025)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2025-11; 36 O.S. § 6050.3
**Effective:** January 1, 2026

Default reimbursement rate (when rates not voluntarily submitted): lesser of **325% of current CMS rate** or billed charges. Consumer cost-sharing cannot exceed in-network patient amounts.

---

### 1.12 STRENGTHEN OKLAHOMA HOMES ACT — HB 3089 (2024)
**Source:** OID Legislative Summary; 36 O.S. §§ 971+
**Effective:** November 1, 2024

Grant program allowing residential property owners to construct or retrofit homes to resist catastrophic weather loss. Property owners must obtain at least 3 bids from certified contractors.

---

### 1.13 VEHICLE STORAGE RATE CAPS — SB 641 (2025)
**Source:** OID Bulletin No. 2025-07; 36 O.S. §§ 1273–1274
**Effective:** November 1, 2025

Maximum storage rates for total loss vehicles:
- Days 1–10: $39/day (standard); $125/day (lithium-ion battery damage)
- Day 11+: $75/day (standard); $200/day (lithium-ion battery damage)

## SECTION 2: OKLAHOMA DOI BULLETINS AND REGULATORY GUIDANCE (2023–2025)

### 2.1 ANTI-REBATING ENFORCEMENT — BULLETIN NO. 2025-01
**Issued:** January 17, 2025

OID found evidence of producers and insurers negotiating returns of commissions to secure/retain business. Prohibited under 36 O.S. § 1204(8)(a). Anti-rebating laws ensure uniform pricing across customers.

**Prohibited:** Returning commissions (or portions) as inducement to write or keep business; offering reduced premiums in exchange for returned commissions.

---

### 2.2 COMMISSION DISCONTINUATION — BULLETIN NO. 2025-12
**Issued:** 2025

OID identified carriers eliminating commissions mid-year and restricting enrollment without advance notice — potential violations of 36 O.S. § 1209(A) unfair trade practices. OID warned enforcement action will follow.

---

### 2.3 ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN INSURANCE — BULLETIN NO. 2024-11
**Issued:** November 14, 2024

All authorized insurers must develop a written AI Systems (AIS) Program covering governance, risk management, third-party oversight, and consumer privacy. AI-driven decisions must comply with Unfair Trade Practices Act and Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act. OID may request AI program documentation during examinations.

---

### 2.4 SURPLUS LINES — SLIP PLATFORM — BULLETIN NO. 2023-12
**Effective:** January 1, 2024

Oklahoma adopted the Surplus Lines Information Portal (SLIP) for reporting and payment of surplus lines premium taxes. All policies effective on or after January 1, 2024 must be reported through SLIP. Premium taxes: **6% surplus lines premium tax** + **0.175% SLAS transaction fee**.

---

### 2.5 MEDICARE SUPPLEMENT — BIRTHDAY RULE — BULLETIN NO. 2023-10
**Rule:** OAC 365:10-5-129(f) and (g)
**Effective:** September 1, 2023

Annual **60-day open enrollment window beginning on the policyholder's birthday** — allows switching to same or lesser Medicare supplement benefits without medical underwriting. Issuers must also notify disability enrollees **60–90 days before their 65th birthday** about open enrollment rights.

---

### 2.6 CE REQUIREMENT — EARTHQUAKE CE ELIMINATED — BULLETIN NO. 2023-05
**Effective:** September 1, 2023

Oklahoma's previously required earthquake CE course has been eliminated. OAC 365:25-3-1 and 365:25-3-14 amended.

---

### 2.7 SHORT-TERM LIMITED DURATION INSURANCE — BULLETIN NO. 2025-08
**Issued:** 2025

Federal non-enforcement of 2024 STLDI restrictions. Oklahoma STLDI may have duration up to **36 months total** per 36 O.S. § 4419(A). Carriers must still obtain OID approval through SERFF before selling under extended duration.

---

### 2.8 OKLAHOMA STATE-BASED EXCHANGE TRANSITION
**Effective:** May 1, 2026 (program administration); November 1, 2027 (full SBE operation)

Oklahoma transitioning from CMS administration to State-Based Exchange on Federal Platform. OID assumes control of user fees, health plan management, navigator programs, and hotline support. Producers selling Marketplace plans will increasingly interface with OID rather than CMS.

## SECTION 4: CONSUMER PROTECTION HIGHLIGHTS

- **Annuity Best Interest (2023):** Strongest new senior protection — four obligations apply to all annuity recommendations
- **Medicare Supplement Birthday Rule (2023):** Annual guaranteed-issue switching right for all Medicare supplement policyholders
- **Anti-Rebating (2025):** OID actively enforcing commission kickback prohibitions
- **AI in Underwriting (2024):** Carriers responsible for AI-driven discriminatory outcomes — producers can flag and report
- **Assignment of Benefits Prohibition (2025):** Eliminates widespread contractor fraud scheme on property claims
- **Claims Refund Window Reduction (2025):** Shorter recoupment windows protect consumers and providers

## SECTION 5: ENFORCEMENT AND DISCIPLINARY

### 5.1 PRODUCER LICENSE ENFORCEMENT — 36 O.S. § 1435.13
OID may place on probation, censure, suspend, revoke, refuse to renew, or levy civil penalties. Grounds include misrepresentation, misappropriation, fraud, dishonest practices, and violating any insurance law or rule. Actions may proceed even after license surrender.

### 5.2 OID ANTI-FRAUD UNIT
Law enforcement arm of OID. Investigates white-collar insurance crimes. Offices in OKC and Tulsa. Report fraud: fraud.stoppers@oid.ok.gov

### 5.3 2025 ENFORCEMENT THEMES
OID issued two bulletins on compensation practices (anti-rebating and carrier commission elimination) — signals active enforcement attention in this area.
