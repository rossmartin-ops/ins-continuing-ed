# HI Content Manifest

State: Hawaii
Tier: 2 (TX Baseline — no state-specific content required)
Requirement: 24 CE hours per 2-year license term
- Ethics: 3 hrs (ethics OR insurance laws & rules)
- Self-study split: No split requirement specified (single-line: 21 hrs in LOA + 3 ethics; dual-line: 10 L/H + 11 P/C + 3 ethics)
- Proctored exam: REQUIRED
- Carryover: Not specified
- CE due: 15 days before renewal date

Line-of-authority breakdown:
- Single-line producers: 21 hrs in line of authority + 3 hrs ethics = 24 hrs
- Dual-line producers: 10 hrs Life/Health + 11 hrs Property/Casualty + 3 hrs ethics = 24 hrs

Notes: TX baseline (23.87 hrs) satisfies the 24-hr total. HI requires a proctored final exam — all self-study courses must be proctored. The TX selection delivers 10.77 hrs of self-study content (LHPRD = 5.41 hrs, L/H focused; PCCOM = 5.36 hrs, P/C focused) which maps well to the dual-line split. Classroom equivalent courses do not require proctoring. Verify proctoring logistics with Hawaii Insurance Division before launch.

## HI State-Specific Notes

- Proctored exam: Hawaii requires a proctored final exam for self-study courses. Platform must integrate with a proctoring provider (e.g., ProctorU, Examity, or equivalent) before HI self-study courses can launch. Classroom equivalent courses are not subject to this requirement.
- CE due date: 15 days before license renewal date (earlier than most states). Ensure learner communication reflects this deadline.
- Ethics requirement: 3 hrs of ethics OR insurance laws & rules. ETH course (2.70 hrs) is the closest available content; verify with Hawaii Insurance Division whether 2.70 credited hours satisfies 3 hrs, or supplement with 0.30 hrs of additional approved content.
- Dual-line P/C shortfall: See Hours Summary above. Need to add ~1.43 hrs of P/C content for dual-line producers meeting the 11-hr P/C minimum.
- No carryover policy confirmed. Verify with Hawaii Insurance Division.
- No state-specific content required — TX baseline is launchable pending Hawaii Insurance Division approval and proctoring integration.
