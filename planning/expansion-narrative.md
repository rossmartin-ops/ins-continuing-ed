# CE Expansion Narrative & Build Plan

Last updated: March 2026

---

## Where We Are

Texas is the first state in active build. The course structure has been defined, chapter cuts are specified in the manifest, and quiz/exam questions are being written. TX is the proof of concept that validates the entire approach.

The repo contains:
- 5 source courses (L-version JSONs, full content library, ~37 hrs total)
- Manifests for all 49 target states (48 + TX)
- State requirements research for all states
- A tiered prioritization of build effort

---

## The 39-State Opportunity

**39 states can be launched with content we already have — no new course writing required.**

These states need only:
1. Jarvis build work (implement chapter cuts per manifest)
2. Quiz checkpoints added to classroom equivalent courses
3. Final exams added to self-study courses

Same playbook as TX. Once the TX build is complete and validated, these states are a replication exercise.

### Tier 1 — Near-identical to TX (4 states)
These share TX's exact 24-hr / 12/12 classroom-self-study structure. Manifests are essentially TX with updated headers.

| State | Key Difference from TX |
|---|---|
| NJ | Up to 12 hrs carryover |
| VT | CE Attestation form required post-completion |
| NC | CE due 60 days before expiration; Flood recurring every 4 yrs |
| UT | No insurer-sponsored courses >12 hrs |

### Tier 2 — 24 hrs, full delivery flexibility (~28 states)
No classroom/self-study split mandate. TX content selection (~23.87 hrs) covers the requirement. State-specific notes documented in each manifest.

ME, NH, RI, PA, DE, MD, WV, SC, GA, AL, MS, TN, KY, AR, LA, SD, OH, MI, MN, ND, NE, WY, ID, WA, AK, HI, NM, DC

Notable flags:
- 5 states require proctored self-study exams (WV, GA, AL, MS, AR, DC, HI) — platform consideration, not a content blocker
- SD requires only 10 hrs (single line) / 20 hrs (dual line)
- GA caps online as self-study (12-hr effective limit — our selection at 10.77 hrs is within cap)

### Tier 3 lower — Non-standard hours, covered by existing content (5 states)

| State | Total Hrs | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| MO | 16 | ETH (2.70) + expanded self-study to reach 16 hrs |
| VA | 16 (single) / 24 (dual) | TX selection works for dual-line; single-line needs trimmed version |
| KS | 18 | Trimmed content selection from TX baseline |
| NV | 30 (3-yr) | Full L-version catalog (~37 hrs) covers requirement |
| IA | 36 (3-yr) | Full L-version catalog (~37 hrs) covers requirement with small buffer |

| State | Required | We Have | Gap |

## Recommended Sequencing

### Phase 1 — Validate the model (now)
- Complete TX build
- Confirm quiz/exam format works in Jarvis
- Get TDI approval

### Phase 2 — Scale with existing content
- Build Tier 1 states (NJ, VT, NC, UT) — near-zero incremental effort beyond TX
- Roll through Tier 2 states in volume — same build pattern, largest group
- Build Tier 3 lower (MO, VA, KS) — minor content selection adjustments
- Build NV and IA using full catalog — no new content, just full L-version builds

### Phase 3 — Higher-hour states
- Decide on MA and AZ — assess market size vs. content investment required
- If yes: identify content gaps and prioritize new course development

### Phase 4 — State-specific content
- Prioritize Tier 4 states by market size / strategic value
- FL and NY are likely highest priority given licensee population
- Build state-specific content modules as standalone add-ons to the existing catalog
