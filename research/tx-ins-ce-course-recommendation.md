# TX INS CE: Course Delivery Method Options + Recommendation

# Recommendation

Given (1) our existing CE courses in Jarvis and the way they align to requirements, (2) competitor delivery methods, (3) the long-term creation of a robust, scaleable, nationally applicable CE catalog and (4) balancing all of those factors with LX capacity and a bias toward fast, efficient course builds, the LX recommendation is to:

* Fulfill the minimum 12-hour classroom equivalent requirement by revising/updating these existing self study courses to meet classroom equivalent requirements
  * Insurance Ethics and Insurance Fraud
  * Life & Health Insurance Options
  * Understanding Personal Lines Property & Casualty Insurance
* Fulfill any remaining hours requirement with self-study hours by adding a final exam to these existing courses
  * Life & Health Products and Compliance
  * Understanding Commercial Property & Casualty Insurance

Note: Our existing CE courses create a 5-course package that covers information for Life, Health, Property and Casualty license types. We do not currently have enough content/courses that would fulfill the hours requirements if we wanted to strictly offer L&H-relevant CE and P&C-relevant CE packages. For H1 2026 we will NOT expand offerings to have L&H or P&C focused CE packages.

# Context/Research

## Overall Requirements

Texas licensed insurance producers and adjusters must complete a set number of CE hours every **two-year license term**:

* **24 CE hours total** for most licenses (life, health, P&C, adjusters, etc.).
  * 3 hours must be in ethics/consumer protection.
  * At least half (12 hours) of the required CE must be classroom or "classroom equivalent."
  * Only up to 12 hours may be self-study.

## Course Delivery Methods

### Classroom Equivalent

An online or remote-delivery CE course that is treated *like a physical classroom* for CE credit purposes.

**Key Features:**

* **Interactive learning** — content is broken into modules or units with **embedded quizzes or checkpoints** rather than a single end-of-course exam.
  * Timed content (content must align with the approved CE hours).
  * Interactive activities or quizzes that promote engagement.
  * Progress tracking and pacing (students may need to complete one section before moving on).

* No final proctored exam — instead, students must **pass quizzes or interactive checks** as they proceed.

* Online classroom-equivalent content *counts toward the classroom requirement* under Texas rules.

**Aceable will be required to offer at least 12 of the 24 hours as classroom equivalent**

### Self Study

Independent study courses that learners complete on their own time, typically with **a final exam**.

**Key Features:**

* Often delivered online, print, or self-paced digital formats.

* Completion is usually validated by a **final exam** (often open-book).

* **No monitoring required** for the exam.

**Maximum of half the total required CE hours** (e.g., up to 12 hours for a 24-hour requirement).
